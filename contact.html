Ken Shenstone Ceramic Studio & Albion Anagama

Visiting

The studio is located in rural Albion, Michigan — about 15 minutes from downtown Albion and roughly an hour west of Ann Arbor. The property includes Ken's studio, the Albion Anagama, three additional kilns, and a 4,000 square foot ceramics gallery.

Studio sales are held after firings, typically in autumn. Pottery is available ranging from $8 to hundreds of dollars.

If you're interested in visiting the kiln, participating in a firing, or learning about the Resident Artist or Apprenticeship programs, contact Ken directly by phone.

For Artists

Ken's firings are collaborative events. If you are a potter interested in firing your work in one of the largest anagama kilns in the world, reach out. Participants typically contribute by bringing meals, splitting wood, or helping supervise the fire during the 10-day cycle, in exchange for space in the kiln.

As of September 2025, Ken was actively seeking apprentices — the studio continues to operate as a living, working center for wood-fired ceramics.

About This Site

Ken's original websites — hosted on Squarespace and referenced at kenshenstone.com — are no longer online. The CultureVerse page documenting his kiln has also gone down. This site was built to preserve and present Ken's legacy using information gathered from web archives, press coverage, community posts, and personal knowledge.

If you have photographs, stories, or corrections to contribute, please get in touch. This is a living document — it gets better with every contribution.
